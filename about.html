I was born and raised in a small town in western Canada called Vernon. After finishing high school, I moved to Vancouver Island to attended the University of Victoria (UVic).

As an undeclared student in the engineering department, I was exposed to coding in Basic, ANSI C, and Java, and I immediately took to it. At the end of my first year, I enrolled in the Bachelor of Software Engineering program, from which I graduated with distinction in 2014.

Next up, San Francisco! After UVic, I accepted an offer at Twitter with the Web Platform team.

I worked at Twitter for 3 years across 4 teams:

  • Web Platform
  • Timelines Client - Web
  • Timelines Client - Android
  • Notifications Client

I learned a lot there and made a ton of life-long friendships, but I knew I wanted to try grad school. So in 2017, I packed up my things and moved across the country to Pittsburgh, PA, where I enrolled in the Masters of Intelligent Information Systems program at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU).

After that, it was back to SF for a stint at Stripe, working at a Machine Learning Engineer on the Merchant Classification team. With this team, I maintained Spark/Scalding data pipelines and trained an merchant embedding model in PyTorch. Using these embeddings, I built a Terms of Service (ToS) violators model and an industry classifier.

However, in 2020, I decided to go back to Android development and took my old job at Twitter again. What a ride!

While I was at Twitter, I made the move across the country yet again. This time, it was my partner's turn to attend grad school.

While in Boston, Twitter imploded which brings me to Etsy and NYC!
